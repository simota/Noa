## cols: 10
## rows: 5
## mode: text
## input:
\e[2;4r\e[?6hAAA\r\nBBB\r\nCCC\r\nDDD\r\nEEE
## expect:
AAA
CCC
DDD
EEE

# cursor: 3,3
## why:
DECSTBM + DECOM. DECSTBM 2;4 confines scrolling to rows 2-4 and homes the
cursor; the LF at the region bottom scrolls only rows 2-4 (BBB is discarded at
the top margin — a partial region never feeds scrollback) while rows outside
the margins stay put. KNOWN GAP: noa tracks DECOM (?6) but does not yet apply
origin-relative addressing, so AAA lands on absolute row 1 instead of the
region top; in xterm/Ghostty row 1 would stay blank and AAA would scroll away.
Re-bless when DECOM addressing lands — the Ghostty oracle will flag this line.
